The Best Shaving Subscriptions For Men | Gillette on Demand VS. Harry's

We thoroughly researched & reviewed the top shaving subscriptions. Here are the results!

Buying a razor can be a harrowing experience. From deciphering what brand you can rely on for quality and precision to selecting the ideal razor model from an endless list of options — each offering different features, results, and guarantees — the process is enough to make one consider leaving their facial hair wild and free, Castaway-style.

And then there are the costs. The device itself is affordable, but the disposable parts are not. A fancy looking, grippy-rubber handle is thrown in to entice you, and you get a measly couple of cartridges with it. Then, when those wear out, you need to buy replacements to keep your supply stocked on a regular basis.

Luckily, the landscape is changing when it comes to razor options, with shaving subscriptions making serious headway. All of this increased competition is a good thing as shaving is, at least for most of us, a daily requirement. The fact that we’ve gotten locked into paying hefty sums just to keep ourselves well-groomed is a travesty. A few manufacturers have taken the fight to the big boys, and they’re making the established brands a bit nervous, and for good reason. Their prices tend to be far more affordable, and the razors no longer look like Klingon rifles, but have simple, classic designs.

What is Harry's

Harry’s is an affordable shaving subscription service that delivers new razors to your doorstep to keep you looking fresh. It all started when Jeff Raider, co-founder of online prescription lens giant Warby Parker, teamed up with his old friend Andy Katz-Mayfield with one goal in mind: to revolutionize the way we shave. They figured it was about time someone took the initiative to bring shaving into the digital age and decided to do just that. With the Warby Parker model in mind, their idea was to take a market that is dominated by a small handful of players selling high-priced products and bring it online to help remove the friction and high costs from distribution, and give the product a sexier look through smarter design.

  • Harry’s is known for its quality and the aesthetic of their products: simple and elegant.
  • Best for men who take pride in their shaving ritual but don’t feel the need to own a $200 razor.
  • When you subscribe to one of their shave plans, Harry’s gives you a razor, a travel-sized shave gel, and a travel blade cover for free.
  • Plan choices are flexible depending on how often you shave (receive razors every two, three, or five months) and what you’d like from Harry’s (just razors or the gel and aftershave too). It’s easy to modify if you find there are too many or not enough products. And they send you an email two days before each shipment, so you can check-in and ensure you will receive the right amount.

Cost

Harry’s offers all shave-plan subscribers a free trial (valued at $13) that includes a Truman handle with attached blade cartridge, a 2-ounce shave gel, and a travel case for your razor. For monthly subscription plans, prices range from $15 every five months for eight blades delivered to your door (for those of you who shave infrequently). Or, you can choose to bundle the eight blades and a full-sized shave gel for $21.

  • Blades Plan (8 Blades): $15/month
  • Blades & Shave Gel Plan (8 Blades & 1 Shave Gel): $21/month

If you’d prefer to get more than the free trial to start, you can purchase one of the starter kits: the $15 Truman set or the $25 Winston set.

Running low on shave gel or shaving cream? Shave gel with aloe will only set you back $4, while Harry’s shaving cream with eucalyptus costs $8.

How to Subscribe

Onboarding with Harry’s is a pleasure. Simply visit Harrys.com and click on ‘Shave Plans.’ Then click on ‘Start a Trial.’

From there, choose your handle color (orange, blue, or green) and decide how often you want your blades shipped. Here, you’ll also be able to pick if you just want the eight blade cartridges or if you want to add on shave gel each time, too. The rest is like any other online shopping experience. Create an account and enter in your address and payment info.

The Account Management page makes it easy for you to make sure your subscription is serving you as it should. They also have great content on site about tips and tricks as well general interest stories too.

What is Gillette On Demand

Formerly known as the Gillette Shave Club, Proctor & Gamble upgraded its subscription service in an aggressive effort to compete with online rivals such as Dollar Shave Club (now owned by Unilever) and Harry’s. Gillette’s research showed 41% of former subscribers complained about getting more blades than needed. Therefore, it decided to revamp (and rename) the service, giving customers “control without having to choose between convenience and flexibility, or between great quality and value.”

Gillette On Demand engages the Generation X community by promoting a contemporary subscription model that is instinctively simple as shooting a text. No refilling of your virtual cart. No re-entering payment information. No back and forth customer service. Razor replenishment at the tip of your finger and delivered right to your door.

  • Gillette currently dominates the global razor business with over an est. 50% market share.
  • The company holds hundreds of patents related to razors and razor technology.
  • On Demand offers the flexibility to customize the delivery schedule.
  • First orders include razor handle and 4-5 blade refills.
  • No commitments or lengthy contracts.
  • Ships to all US and military locations via APO/FPO/DPO address at no extra cost (depending on the subscription model).

Cost

Gillette offers three razor subscriptions. Sitting in the lower tier is the Sensor3 system starting at $8.00. Right in the middle is the Mach3 Turbo starting at $10. Then at the top comes the ProShield starting at $18.45. Keep in mind these prices are fixed for just the first order. Every other box moving forward comes at the original MSRP, which is $3 extra on all models. A $2 shipping charge is applied to Sensor3 boxes, whereas the other two come with free shipping. All subscriptions are auto-renewed.

How to Subscribe

Start by selecting a razor plan. Then choose between a service: On Demand or Set It & Forget It. The first option is only a one-time purchase. Obviously, you want a subscription, so settle for the latter. Here you can select to have razors shipped at a set time frame every 1-6 months. Move forward in creating an account by punching in an email address and creating a password. Make it a strong one or Gillette won’t grant you access. Once passed this stage, you’ll be requested to submit a billing/shipping address. Then comes credit card info and placing the order.
